Introduction to Key Biomarkers

Systemic inflammation is triggered by the activation of specific signaling molecules known as biomarkersmeasurable substances in the body that indicate a biological state or condition. These biomarkers are crucial in diagnosing and monitoring systemic inflammation:

  • C-reactive Protein (CRP)
  • Interleukin-6 (IL-6)
  • Tumor Necrosis Factor-alpha (TNF-α)
  • Interleukin-1 beta (IL-1β)

1. C-reactive Protein (CRP)

CRP is an acute-phase protein produced by the liver in response to the acute phase response, mediated by IL-6. Displays several functions associated with host defense: it promotes agglutination, bacterial capsular swelling, phagocytosis and complement fixation through its calcium-dependent binding to phosphorylcholine. Can interact with DNA and histones and may scavenge nuclear material released from damaged circulating cells. (UniProt, 2024) Its levels rise during systemic inflammation, making it a key marker for identifying inflammatory responses.

2. Interleukin-6 (IL-6)

IL-6 is a pro-inflammatory cytokine with a wide variety of biological functions in immunity, tissue regeneration, and metabolism. Binds to IL6R, then the complex associates to the signaling subunit IL6ST/gp130 to trigger the intracellular IL6-signaling pathway. (Uniprot,2024) It promotes the differentiation of B cells and the production of acute-phase proteins like CRP.

3. Tumor Necrosis Factor-alpha (TNF-alpha)

TNF-alpha is a pro-inflammatory cytokine and central mediator of inflammation, activating pathways like NF-κB protein complex controlling DNA transcription and cytokine production. to promote cytokine production and inflammation.

4. Interleukin-1 (IL-1)

Pro-inflammatory cytokine and potend pyrogen. IL-1 triggers inflammation by recruiting immune cells and promoting other pro-inflammatory cytokines like IL-6 and TNF-alpha. It also causes fever through its action on the hypothalamus.

Common Features of These Biomarkers

  • Inflammatory Response: All biomarkers play a role in initiating or propagating inflammation.
  • Acute-Phase Response: CRP and cytokines like IL-6 regulate acute-phase proteins.
